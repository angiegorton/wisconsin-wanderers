Enter Science Hall if You Dare

(Or if You Have Class There)

Science Hall. We all know the one. That creepy red building right next to Memorial Union on UW Madison’s campus. To some, it's another building that houses their respective discussions and lecture halls. To others, it's a vessel for insidious forces. Surprisingly, I never had a reason to go into Science Hall, even after being on campus for 2 years. That is until now, when I braved the building on a random Tuesday night.

First things first, let’s address the allegations. According to this UW Madison alumni , tons of strange occurrences have historically gone down in Science Hall. Which for the record has a misleading name since the only people populated in Science Hall since the fifties are the cartography and geography students. Regardless, some of these incidents include a mad scientist with an attic laboratory, strange feelings in the hallways, the sounds of footsteps, and the like. Again, just cliché heebee jeebee jargon with not a lot of facts to back it up. The only true story I could find was that at one point in 1974 some cartography students were cleaning a room on the 4th floor, found a way inside the attic, and discovered an embalmed foot. The dramatics of this story are dispelled when you realize anatomy students used to occupy the upper floors of Science Hall back in the day and just did a poor job when it came to moving all of their materials after relocating to a different building on campus.

My thoughts on Science Hall? The architecture is fascinating to look at. I walked around almost every square inch of the building I could find. There’s a lot of strange little half doors littered throughout the building. Sure, they were a little weird. I can’t think of any explanation for why they exist, and couldn’t get any closure since every door I tried to open was locked. But still, this was not enough to warrant a haunting for me. There was a pretty sick golden elevator I rode to the 4th floor. From there you can see the attic lab of the “Mad Scientist”, but it's inaccessible because the stairs to get up there were locked. The most bizarre thing I saw was a door in between the third and fourth floors. It was half sized like the other ones, and regardless of the fact that I have no idea how anyone would get up there in the first place, I’m sure that it was also locked.

At the end of the day, my verdict is a no-go to there being hauntings in Science Hall. I think it's more eerie as an architectural anomaly than the supposed supernatural forces in place. And in my opinion, there’s worse buildings to reside in on campus (see: crusty Computer Sciences building, the brutalist architecture of Mosse Humanities, or the archaic old elevator in Slichter Residence Hall).
